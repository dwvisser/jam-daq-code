Histogram Menu

New...
The user creates a new histogram and adds it to the drop-down list. Name is a short (up to 16 characters) name for the histogram to go on the dropdown list. Title is a longer descriptive name for the histogram, which is displayed in the plot panel and on printouts. Type is either a 1 or a 2, the dimensionality of the histogram. Size is the number of channels in the histogram, and is usually a power of two. For example, to make a 1024 channel histogram, the user would enter 1024 in Size and the resulting histogram will have channels numbered 0 thru 1023.
Zero...
Three buttons are on this dialog. Displayed will zero the currently displayed histogram. All will zero all histograms in memory. Cancel lets one quit the dialog without zeroing anything. Warning: there is no safety feature on this. Once you click a button, the histogram contents are gone!
Delete...
Completely remove the currently displated histogram.
Calibrate...
Calibrate the displayed histogram, either by entering coefficients or asking for a fit. Each histogram can store its own calibration function.
Projections...
Project a 2-d histogram into a 1-d histogram. The entire histogram, a slice, or the contents of a gate may be projected. The destination histogram may be an existing histogram or a new one which you name.
Combine...
Add, subtract, multiply or divide a pair of 1-d histograms into either a new histogram you name, or an existing histogram. You may also renormalize an existing histogram (into a destination histogram) by multiplying the bin counts by a constant.
Gain Shift...
Apply a gain shift to the displayed histogram into a user-specified destination histogram. Area is conserved.
